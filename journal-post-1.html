Struggle after long break

March 10, 2025

Six months isn't that short—it's actually quite long for a dancer. If you stop dancing for one day, it's okay. Two days? Fine. Maybe even a week. But once you stop for longer than that, your body starts to feel weird, and your mindset changes compared to when you're dancing regularly.

When I got injured in August, I almost quit dancing. I felt like I'd never dance again because my knees and back hurt so much. For the past half-year, at least one part of my body always felt off. I couldn't dance, so I stayed away from the local dance scene, my dancing friends, and even my coach. Sometimes, I didn't even have the courage to watch their Instagram stories because I knew they'd be posting dance videos—and I couldn't join.

Another concern was judgment. I know I can start again whenever I want since I'm not a professional dancer—I don't rely on this for a living. But I was afraid people would think, "She used to dance so well—what happened to her now? She's just average, or even below." Dancing was my thing—my specialty. It made me feel like I was better than others, and I didn't want to lose that. Maybe it's a narcissistic thing, I don't know.

It took me a long time to finally go back to the dance studio. Just stepping inside again took me a month. Last week, I took my first class since November. I wouldn't say it felt good—just normal. And today, I practiced the piece I learned last week. I felt okay—not like before, but at least I wasn't being hard on myself anymore.

The most important thing today was freestyling to that song. It's by Catherine, a choreographer from Australia—she's amazing, with that groovy, water-like movement. She used it in a solo freestyle video (in black and white), and I wanted to try something similar. I wanted to see what would come out of it—if something surprising would happen.

And it did. I've never felt so emotional while dancing before. Usually, I'm just thinking about the moves, the beats, whether I'm doing it right. But this time, I didn't know how to start. I stood there for a while, unsure. Then I told myself, "Just move one arm, then the other, then your leg—don't overthink it."

I went to the ground and stayed there for a long time. Usually floorwork is not my thing, but this time, I felt so connected to the earth—so close to nature, almost spiritual. Then I stood up, leaned against the wall, and got really emotional. I think I even cried a little.

When I watched the video back, some movements still felt awkward, but I'm trying to accept that part of myself. At this stage in my life, I don't plan to become a professional dancer or rely on this for money. It's just a hobby—something personal, meaningful, and fun. Of course, I'll keep taking classes, especially once I feel better. But I also want to spend more time exploring movement with myself and friends.

Yeah, that's it for today.
